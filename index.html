The Product

Understanding SunSteel

Product Overview

What Is SunSteel?

SunSteel™ is a factory-integrated solar roofing system built around AUO's high-efficiency solar modules and Cornerstone's Double-Lok™ standing seam panels. Solar is integrated at the factory — not assembled at the job site.

The SunSteel Panel

SunSteel™ integrated solar roofing system  |  Developed by AUO and Panel Clad Inc.  |  U.S. Patent No. US-20260088750-A1

Manufacturing

How It's Made

Cornerstone manufactures the standing seam panel. AUO integrates the solar module and brackets using a proprietary cold-forming process with no roof penetrations. The completed SunSteel roof panel ships directly to the job site — ready to install.

Installation

On the Job Site

Because solar is already integrated, installation follows the same workflow as typical standing seam roofing. The roofing contractor installs the panels as usual — an electrician simply makes the electrical connections between panels and runs conduit and wiring from the roof to the inverter.

Economics

How Solar Saves Money

Commercial solar reduces utility bills by generating on-site power during peak demand hours — when rates are highest. Most commercial buildings see payback periods of 5–8 years depending on rate structure, system size, and available incentives, with solar production and savings typically extending for well more than 20 years.

Incentives

Tax Credits & Depreciation

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) provides a 30% federal tax credit on the cost of your solar project. Combined with accelerated depreciation (bonus depreciation under MACRS), commercial building owners can offset well over half of the total project cost — often the deciding factor in a customer's decision to move forward.

Technology

How Solar Works

Solar panels convert sunlight into DC electricity. An inverter converts that to AC power that the building can use. Excess power feeds back to the grid (net metering) or to battery storage for later use.

Sizing

System Sizing Basics

System size is determined by roof area and energy usage. SunSteel Services provides preliminary sizing from basic building dimensions and expected energy usage — based on building size, type, and occupancy — no utility bills required for a first estimate.

Interconnection

Connecting to the Grid

Before a commercial solar system can operate, it must be permitted and approved by the local utility for grid interconnection. SunSteel Services supports the full interconnection process on every project.

Financing

Financing Options

SunSteel can be financed as part of a construction loan on new builds, or through dedicated solar financing for retrofit projects. In many cases, a loan can be structured so that energy savings and tax benefits exceed monthly payments from day one — making SunSteel cash-flow positive at the outset.

For Building Owners

Solar That's Part of Your Roof

SunSteel integrates solar into your metal building roof from the start — not as an afterthought.

What It Means for You

A Smarter Way
to Add Solar

Most commercial solar is added to a building after the roof is already complete — a separate trade, a separate timeline, and a separate set of penetrations that can compromise your roof warranty and delay your project.

SunSteel is different. The solar modules are integrated into the standing seam roof panels at the factory, before the product ships to your job site. Your roof is the solar system — delivered and installed by the same trusted contractor building your project.

That means one trade, one timeline, and one point of accountability. And because your project moves faster, you start saving money on energy costs sooner.

Ask your contractor about SunSteel, or contact us to learn more.

Streamlined Project Delivery

Roof and solar are purchased, designed, and installed together — faster and simpler than managing multiple contractors with separate scopes and timelines.

Single-Point Coordination

One contractor is responsible for both roof and solar. That reduces the risk of disputes, delays, finger-pointing, and warranty issues that come with multiple trades on the same project.

Higher Quality, Lower Maintenance Risk

Factory preassembly improves consistency and eliminates roof penetrations. Fewer parts and no aftermarket mounting hardware means fewer long-term maintenance issues.

Upfront Solar Adoption

Integrating solar from the start meets sustainability goals at project outset and avoids future retrofit costs, power shutdowns, and the disruption of adding solar to an occupied building later.

Clean Integration & Aesthetics

SunSteel's low-profile design is integrated into the roof itself — delivering a uniform, architectural appearance that traditional rack-mounted systems cannot match.

Financial Benefits

Significant Economics
from Day One

30% Federal Tax Credit — On the Full Project Cost

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) provides a 30% federal tax credit on the cost of your solar project. Because SunSteel is an integrated solar roof product, customers may qualify for a higher tax credit than with standalone solar. Confirm eligibility with your CPA or tax advisor.

Accelerated Depreciation

In addition to the ITC, commercial solar projects qualify for accelerated depreciation (bonus depreciation under MACRS). Combined with the tax credit, these incentives can offset well over half of the total project cost — significantly improving payback and return on investment.

Built for Commercial & Industrial Construction

SunSteel is built specifically for pre-engineered metal buildings and industrial metal roofing — not retrofitted from residential solar technology. It's engineered from the ground up for commercial and industrial construction.
